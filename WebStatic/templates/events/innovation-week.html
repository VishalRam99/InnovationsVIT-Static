Innovation Week 2026

Join our nation-wide hackathon celebrating creativity, collaboration, and cutting-edge solutions.

Hackathon Rules & Guidelines

Event: Inter-College Hackathon @ VIT Vellore — Innovation Week 2026

Duration: 2 Days (48 Hours)

Mode: Onsite Hackathon (Hardware & Software Tracks)

Domains / Tracks

Participants can innovate in any of the following areas:

  1. Artificial Intelligence (AI) / Machine Learning (ML)
  2. Robotics
  3. Internet of Things (IoT)
  4. Augmented Reality (AR) / Virtual Reality (VR)
  5. Open Innovation

Team Formation Rules

  • Each team must consist of exactly 4 members.
  • All team members must belong to the same college (no cross-college teams allowed).
  • Each participant can join only one team.
  • Participants must be currently enrolled students of a recognized college or university.

Event Format

Day 1

  • Registration and problem statement announcement.
  • Idea pitching and mentor sessions.
  • Development and prototyping phase begins.

Day 2

  • Continuation of development and mentor reviews.
  • Final submission, demo, and judging.
  • Valedictory and prize distribution ceremony.

Project Guidelines

  • Projects can be hardware-based, software-based, or hybrid.
  • Solutions must be developed entirely during the hackathon period.
  • Use of open-source tools, frameworks, and models is allowed (with proper credits).
  • Each team should maintain a GitHub (or similar) repository for submissions.
  • A demo and short presentation are mandatory during the final judging round.

Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on:

  1. Innovation & Originality – Novelty of the idea and creative thinking.
  2. Technical Implementation – Quality, functionality, and complexity of the solution.
  3. Impact & Practicality – Usefulness, scalability, and real-world relevance.
  4. Design & User Experience – Clarity, accessibility, and polish of the prototype.
  5. Presentation & Teamwork – Delivery, communication, and coordination during demo.

General Rules

  • Participants must carry their valid college ID cards.
  • Cross-college teams are not permitted.
  • Plagiarized or pre-built projects will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Teams must adhere to the submission deadlines strictly.
  • The decision of the judging panel will be final and binding.
  • Participants must uphold professional and respectful conduct at all times.
  • Accommodation and meals may be provided for outstation participants (details will follow).

Awards & Recognition

  • Attractive cash prizes, goodies, and internship opportunities for top teams.
  • Certificates of Participation for all eligible teams.

Special recognitions for:

  • Best Hardware Project
  • Best Software Project
  • Most Innovative Solution
